Our Iconic 1965 Shelby GT350

The 1965 Ford Mustang Shelby GT350 is a high-performance variant of the classic Ford Mustang, modified and refined by Carroll Shelby's team to transform an already popular pony car into a fierce, track-ready machine. It was built with the intention of homologating the Mustang for competition in the Sports Car Club of America's (SCCA) B-Production racing.The 1965 Ford Mustang GT-350 is a highly sought-after muscle car with a special brake package consisting of Kelsey-Hayes 11-inch discs up front and big drum brakes at the rear borrowed from the Ford station wagon line. The Mustang GT350 starts with the 271-hp “High-Performance” version of the 289 V-8 and adds a high-riser intake manifold, welded-tube headers, and cast-aluminum valve-rocker covers which raise peak power to 306.There were 34 "GT350R" race-spec cars built specifically for competition use under SCCA rules, and the model was the B-Production champion for three straight years.
